## Core Description

AI-powered search replaced the 10 blue links. Homeowners type "best roofer" into a chat assistant and get 3 names back. Reviews, citations, and how a company's details are marked up across the web decide which companies appear in the answer. Roofers, dentists, HVAC contractors, and auto shops compete block by block for those slots. Only 1.2% of local firms show up.

Answerspot shows service operators what AI platforms say when customers look for their category in a given city. Owners enter a company name and location, and the dashboard reveals which competitors get recommended, where the firm ranks, and what signals are missing. Week-over-week diffs flag position shifts, new entrants, and the citation changes behind them. Each report carries concrete fixes: review volume targets, citation sources to claim, and markup corrections to submit.

Category queries run through official APIs where offered, scheduled scripted checks everywhere else; results get parsed and stored in Postgres. Checks run manually for 10 beta customers to validate what drives action before automating the query runs, parsing, and report generation. A browser extension lets owners trigger a live lookup between weekly reports. Success means users acting on at least one recommended fix within 7 days.

Local firms spend $1,000 per month on SEO and review management without knowing how AI represents them. Answerspot slots in at $99 per month as an added layer on top of existing spend. Growth runs through SEO-focused Facebook groups, YouTube creators covering AI discovery, and agencies reselling white-label reports. Agencies need a fresh monthly deliverable as clients ask what replaces SEO; this report fits that slot. Expansion deepens into automated fix implementation and agency-tier reporting. The firm that wins an AI slot holds it long before competitors notice the race started.
